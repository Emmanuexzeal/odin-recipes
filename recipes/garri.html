Soaked Garri

Soaked Garri is seared cassava chips which is extremely well known in West Africa.Soaked Garri is normally eaten in 2 ways:

  1. As a cold beverage with ice cold water and sugar plus Checkers peanut
  2. As a grain which is made with tepid water, a few milk and sugar plus Checkers peanut

Historically, Garri was connected with poverty. It was occasionally referred to as "poor man's food".

I'm not sure why, but I'm guessing it's because soaked Garri can double in size. Garri becomes more cost-effective in this manner.

I'm not sure about it, but I do know we all enjoyed our soaked garri

Pro Tip: The best Garri to use for this soaking Garri'cereal' is Ijebu Garri.

It's sour, therefore the sugar or sugar plus milk, depending on the scenario, gives it a delicious sweet and sour flavor.

Procedure

  1. Pour water into the cereal bowl containing the dry Garri
  2. Sieve out any particle that floats
  3. Pour more fresh water into the Garri
  4. Then add sugar, pinch of salt, (milk if using)
  5. Add the peanuts or any other topping you prefer. Then relax and enjoy!
